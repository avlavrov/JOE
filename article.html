TIRR: a potential front runner in HDR race − hypotheses and perspectives

A. A. Anuchina, A. V. Lavrov & S. A. Smirnikhina

Research Center for Medical Genetics

The majority of CRISPR-Cas9 methods for mutations correction are oriented on gene editing through homologous recombination that is normally restrained by non-homologous end joining (NHEJ). A recently identified protein TIRR can bind a 53BP1 protein, a key effector of NHEJ, and inhibit its recruitment to double-strand break loci. Several studies elucidated the molecular mechanisms of TIRR-53BP1 binding and established bidirectional role of TIRR in 53BP1 functions and stability. It was proved that overexpression of TIRR promotes the double-strand break repair through homologous recombination. All findings, which were described in the review, allow assuming TIRR as a suitable target for enhancing efficacy of genome editing through homology directed repair.

Introduction

 

Modern genome editing approaches can be directed to implementation or correction of mutation in nucleotide sequence. In the last case, it is necessary to provide a specific repair of a DNA lesion after Cas9 action. This mechanism is called homology directed repair (HDR) and based on matrix-dependent reconstruction of the double-strand break through homologous recombination (HR)[1]. With the use of specific guide RNA and donor molecule, it is theoretically possible to repair any interesting mutation. The major problem is low efficacy of HDR in comparison to other repair mechanism – non-homologous end-joining (NHEJ), in particular, to classical NHEJ or c-NHEJ [2][3]. HDR and c-NHEJ dominate in different cell-cycle phases. It is proved that c-NHEJ is major repair mechanism in G1 and early G2 phases, while HDR - in late G2 and S phases, because just in these phases homological chromosome – the necessary matrix for HR – is available [4]. Consequently, DSB repair favors more с-NHEJ, that HDR.

A number of methods are used for increase HR efficacy in repair of Cas9-induced break. Particularly, it is cell-cycle synchronization[5], knockdown of NHEJ factors with siRNAs[6], overexpression of HDR factors or their fusion with Cas9[7][8], using different types of nucleases and donor molecules(Byrne, Ortiz, Mali, Aach, & Church, 2015; Zhang et al., 2017;Richardson, Ray, DeWitt, Curie, & Corn, 2016)[12]. Nevertheless, studies with the same methods often demonstrate controversial results. Moreover, many repair stages are not fully studied, new factors are discovered eventually, and that affects and alters the existing repair network [13].

In 2017, a new uncharacterized protein, that involves c-NHEJ-repair pathway, was discovered [14]. TIRR (Tudor-interacting repair regulator) participates in first stages of c-NHEJ, and interacts with its key factor, p53-binding protein 1 (53BP1).  During the c-NHEJ initiation, 53BP1 binds with DSB via histone marks (H4K20me2) and (H2AK15ub)[15] The unique ability of TIRR is to mask the H4K20Me2 binding surface, and “absorb” 53BP1 molecules. It was shown that, on the one hand, TIRR depletion negatively impact 53BP1-stability and protein levels and, on the other hand, TIRR overexpression suppresses 53BP1-chromatin binding. [14][16]These data allow to speak about dual role of TIRR in NHEJ and HR processes[17].[S1]  -.

In the present review general scheme of TIRR involvement in repair pathways is shown, molecular features of protein and their impact on 53BP1 are observed, and advantages of using TIRR as new target for genome editing efficacy improving are discussed.

 

Counteraction of key DSB repair mechanisms in the break locus

 

The choice between c-NHEJ and HDR occurs via complicated network of interactions oriented on promotion of 53BP1 (c-NHEJ key factor) or BRCA1 (HDR key factor) activity. These two proteins are in permanent counterstrategy to each other in the locus of DSB, and both of them mediate many additional factors to stay on the break point.

NHEJ initiation participants:[S2] 

·       53BP1 – is the homooligomer, which main function is to protect the break ends from the initial stage of HR - end resection[18]. It specifically binds with chromatin to dimethylated Lys20 of histone 4 (H4K20me2) with Tudor domain and to RNF168-ubiquitylated H2AK15 with UDR (ubiquitination-dependent recruitment) motif[15], [19].

·       The main effector proteins for 53BP1 are RIF1 (RAP1-interacting factor 1) and PTIP (PAX transactivation activation domain-interacting protein)[20], [21]. The bond between them and 53BP1 becomes available after phosphorylation with ATM (ataxia-telangiectasia mutated)-kinase[22]. 

·       RIF1 mediate recruitment large complex, discovered in 2018 and called Shieldin[23]. It consists of 4 parts: SHLD1, SHLD2, SHLD3 proteins and MAD2L2 protein, which is considered to be the crucial participant of the Shieldin complex and key regulator of its stability[24], [25].

HDR initiation participants:

·       BRCA1 – is a protein that participates in many processes: DNA transcription, cell signaling, chromatin organization, maintaining genomic stability[26]. The protein contains several domains participated in homologous recombination by activating DNA-end resection – the first step of HR[27].

·       MRN – is a complex that consists of Mre11, Rad50 and Nbs1 proteins in eukaryotes[28]. Together with BRCA1 it promotes the initial HR stage - generation of 5′ resected ends, where the main role belongs to MRE11 endonuclease, which nicks the DNA upstream from the break[29]. The actual 3′→5′ resection towards the break is also provided by Mre11, and following deeper resection is performed by two nucleases: Exo1 and Dna2[30][31].  Additionally, a protein CtIP works in cooperation with the MRN complex and is responsible for enhancing end-resection[31].

·       SCAI – is a protein that promotes 53BP1-RIF1 dissociation and which knockdown inhibits the recruitment of main HR-factors (BRCA1, CtIP, etc.) to damage sites[31]. SCAI-RIF1-relations are considered to be the part of the BRCA1 activity.

·       POH1 – is a deubiquitinating enzyme (DUB) that promotes a clearance of 53BP1 from the damage site[32]. It is a proteasome component with ability to counteract RNF8/RNF168 (E3 ubiquitin ligases) dependent ubiquitin activity, which promotes recruitment of 53BP1 and RAP80 (suppresses HR)  proteins to damage site[33]. The  BRCA1-mediated activity of POH1 performs release the break site from RAP80,ubiquitin chains and, consequently, removal the 53BP1[34].

In general, 53BP1-complex act as a permanent inhibitor of end-resection via MRN, CtIP and BRCA1, and provides the protection of downstream NHEJ effectors. For HR initiation BRCA1-system should impair existing barrier stage by stage through the bunch of mechanisms. To sum up, BRCA- and 53BP1-mechanisms are complicated systems, which have several antagonized points and many factors, involved in their stability, thus, many targets to manipulate with efficacy one or another pathway.

 

The role of chromatin condition in repair pathway choice

 

Over last decade a picture of DSB histone marks was enriched by new findings. It was showed that replication status of the DNA can ensure the correct repair pathway via epigenetic marks – different histone modifications can serve as a dock for recruitment different factors[35]. There are evidences about participation several chromatin marks in repair events. Indeed, H3K9me3 is considered to be the initial functionally relevant event in HR-directed gene modification via opening chromatin and recruiting the appropriate enzymes[36]. H3K36me2 mediates c-NHEJ pathway, and H3K36me3 is necessary for HR[37], [38]. H3K4 methylation may be associated with c-NHEJ or HR depending on different type of modifying enzyme.[39]

Interesting results were received in study of Simonetta et al. Two-fold dilution of H4K20me2 mark [S3] was observed in replicated DNA in comparison to non-replicated[40]. Taking into account that dimethylated [S4] [АА5] lysine 20 of histone 4 recruits 53BP1-RIF1 complex and promotes c-NHEJ, decrease in number of corresponding marks provide the release of chromatin from non-homologous factors and mediate access of BRCA1. At the same time, recruitment of 53BP1 to chromatin should be provided only with prepared H2AK15ub mark in combination with H4K20me2[19].

Described evidences considered that repair pathway choice in some extent depends on replication status of chromatin. Nevertheless, despite some histone marks is showed to be involved in repair pathways, correct work of all system can be ensured just with complex action of all epigenetic modifications.

 

The first histone-masking protein

 

Structure and homology

Tudor-interacting repair regulator (Syndesmos or Nudt16L1) was identified and characterized in 2017 by Drane et al. and Zhanget al It has an ability to interact with specific Tudor domain of 53BP1-protein and regulate its activity.[14], [16]

TIRR has a homology to the members of nudix hydrolase family, widely spread in bacteria, viruses, eukaryotes, and archaea, and has 46% sequence homology with its member Nudt16[41]. Nudix proteins have a specific Nudix motif [S6] or box consists of 23 amino acids, and perform different functions in regulation, protection and signaling[42]. They are characterized by ability to remove the organic pyrophosphates from the cellular environment.

Nucleoside Diphosphate-Linked Moiety X Motif 16 – is a pyrophosphatase that hydrolyses nucleic acid substrates and catalyzes removal the cap structure from mRNAs[43]. Nudt16 was reported as evolutionary ancestor of TIRR, which originated from hydrolase enzyme due to DNA mutations[44].  Several studies compared TIRR and Nudt16 and revealed their similarities and differences. First of all, TIRR has a structural resemblance with Nudt16 and save the canonical Nudix fold, but at the same time lacks an intact NUDIX domain, that is why it is not able to perform hydrolase’s functions(Picture)[45]. In turn, Nudt16 is not able to directly interact with 53BP1. One the one hand[S7] , it was showed that Nudix domain is not important in the binding between 53BP1 and TIRR[45]. One the other hand, structure analysis revealed that key residues needed for Tudor domain-interaction are conserved in Nudt16 (including Lys10, Pro105, and Arg107), except one, histidine residue H106(Picture). The residue presents only in L8-loop of TIRR. This loop is shorter in Nudt16, what is supposed to impact Tudor-binding features. Disrupted binding with Tudor is also determined by Nudt16-specific residues: R5, L7, and V100. It was discovered that 53BP1-TIRR binding surface is centered on Arg107 residue. The other important residues from the α1-β1 loop, N-terminus loop and β4-β5 loop of TIRR form a specific hydrophobic layer protecting the hydrophobic dimethyl-lysine binding pocket of Tudor, through which TIRR and H4K20Me2 interacts with TIRR[46].

All these interactions make, a coreand binding surface for stable and effective interaction between 53BP1 and TIRR proteins.

 

Role in DNA repair

Performed studies learned behavior the c-NHEJ and HR factors in TIRR-overexpression, TIRR depletion and next restoring of levels. As markers of restored HR in BRCA1-deficient cells PARP inhibitors (PARPi) were used.  These substances cause multiple double-strand breaks and, if they cannot be correctly repaired, it leads to death of the cell[47]. In opposite, restoring the resistance to PARPi points on the recovery of HR mechanism[48].

TIRR expression was shown to abolish a 53BP1 foci formation. However, there are controversial results about overexpression consequences: Wang et al showed that ectopic overexpression in BRCA1-deficient cells did not affect the sensitivity to PARPi, though normal expression regained HR and decrease the sensitivity to PARP inhibitor treatment. And Drane et al demonstrate that TIRR over-expression in cells with hypomorphic Brca1-alleles [S8] [U9] allowed resistance to the PARP inhibitor olaparib, what can be related to loss of 53BP1 function.Additionally, it was shown that ATM-phosphorylation mediates dissociation of TIRR from 53BP1 that correlates to stage of RIF1-binding[14].

The bidirectional nature of Tudor-interacting repair regulator related to the same effects when TIRR is depleted. Drane et al showed that absence of TIRR increased the binding 53BP1 with different partner molecules, making it more active compared to cells with normal TIRR expression. Also, TIRR depletion increases the 53BP1 foci formation. Wild-type TIRR can restore the DSB-resistant phenotype of the cell, but not the mutant protein, that is not able to bind 53BP1. Other work also demonstrate reduce the numbers of HR-foci in TIRR knockout cells[15].

That study was supplemented with Zhang et al results, who showed that loss of TIRR correlates with reduced 53BP1 expression and restores the resistance to PARP inhibition in BRCA1-deficient cells. The expression of gRNA-resistant wild-type TIRR restored 53BP1 expression and decrease PARPi sensitivity in TIRR-depleted cells.

To explain the dual results, Dranéet al proposed a theory about constitutive level of TIRR, when depletion or increase in levels negatively affects c-NHEJ. Despite the several opposite results, all researchers suggest the inhibitory effect of TIRR on 53BP1 recruitment to damaged chromatin.

 

 

 

 

TIRR for genome editing: perspectives

 

During the last years different approaches for improving HR-mediated genome editing were performed. Inhibition of c-NHEJ for increasing the HR extent is one of the widespread methods. Different repair factors, from the downstream proteins to the key regulators of DSB repair pathway choice were used in a number of appropriate experiments.

The last stage of c-NHEJ is ligation of ends via Ligase IV enzyme in cooperation with XRCC4 and XLF proteins. The Ligase IV inhibition is frequent method for HR enhancing. One of the most usable small molecule for that approach is SCR7[49][50]–[53].It acts in a concentration-dependent manner and blocks the binding of Ligase IV to the break ends and, thus, inhibit the whole c-NHEJ pathway. Other methods for Ligase IV suppression include small RNA molecules action through RNA-interference and proteasomal degradation of the protein[6].

               Another strategy is using different inhibitors of Ku and DNA-PKs, those are crucial for next activation of nucleases, polymerases and ligases. DNA-PKs activity can be suppressed through pharmacological inhibitors (KU-0060648 or ScFv18-2), while Ku can be inhibit through knocking down or using a specific compounds[51], [54].

          Finally, the 53BP1 activity can be also suppressed. An engineered ubiquitin variant, i53 was discovered after screening of corresponding library. Interesting, that it acts similar to TIRR, through binding with Tudor domain and blocking the accumulation in DSB sites[55].

Nowadays we have many methods for HR enhancing and many factors that is why the problem is not the development an approach, but the choice of the correct factor for that. Theoretically, a suitable way of genome editing improving should combine the c-NHEJ inhibition and HR enhancing ways. That can be reached through focusing on the repair pathway choice. NUDT16L1 protein combines several features that makes it a good target for genome editing experiments.

First, TIRR is called as “bona-fide” inhibitor of 53BP1, a natural protein that promotes strong suppression of 53BP1 without insertion into the cell alien molecules. Second, it is the unique bidirectional features of TIRR. These features was explained through the one hypothesis after finding that both 53BP1 and TIRR undergo conformational alteration during binding[56]. The authors supposed that these alterations could lead to inaccessibility the ubiquitination-dependent recruitment (UDR) motif for degradation enzymes. However, in the case of TIRR depletion the motif is in other, available conformation, what results in strong decreasing of 53BP1 protein levels.

Thirdly, using molecules and agents with unknown interactions with other participants of repair pathway lead to possible mistakes in that work. Last studies about TIRR and other repair proteins allow us to construct a scheme of factor interactions in the break loci (pic.)

During DSB formation, 53BP1 undergo several modifications. First, it is a dissociation from TIRR that is mediated by RIF1. Botuyan et al assumed that this step could be interacted with non-coding RNA molecules. It was recently shown that these molecules are present on DSB focus after break induction. It was proposed, that ncRNAs could theoretically interact with both TIRR and RIF1 as RNA-binding proteins[44]. RIF1 can binds with RNA and transport it to TIRR-53BP1 complex, thus modulating its dissociation through translocation the ncRNA on TIRR. At the same time, 53BP1 is phosphorylated by ATM-kinase for being able to directly bond with RIF1 and go to the break locus[14]. After binding with chromatin, 53BP1-RIF1 recruits the Shieldin complex with MAD2L2 and strengthen the stability of c-NHEJ-factors. After that, non-homologous end-joining process prolongs to downstream factors.

In the case of TIRR depletion, we will observer a hyperactive form of 53BP1, as was described[56]. That form have the increasing efficacy of the binding with partners, but also less stability, that will be results in many unstable 53BP1-focuses, that can bind to each H4K20Me2 histone mark, in the DSB or not in the DSB loci, leading to lack of the 53BP1 at the DSB and preventing RIF-1 and Shieldin recruitment. As Shieldin complex is responsible for stability of c-NHEJ focus, longer staying other non-homologous factors in DSB without Shielding should firstly lead to[S10]  dissociation of RIF through phosphorylation of SCAI-binding sites and consequent displacement of RIF1 by SCAI, and secondly, to the removing the 53BP1 through POH1-activity(pic).

TIRR overexpression can lead also to the lack of the 53BP1 in DSB loci, but for the reason of excessive amount of TIRR, molecules that can stronger compete with RIF1. Nevertheless, the consequences should lead to the similar c-NHEJ inhibition..

 

Conclusion

Thus, a theoretical scheme of TIRR interactions, based on variety of experimental studies, confirms the crucial role of TIRR protein as a potential natural swithcer [S11] between c-NHEJ and HR pathways. It action directly linked to only 53BP1 and RIF1 proteins, that excludes the multiple pathways impairment during manipulations with gene-editing efficacy enhancing. Obviously, TIRR can be used in experiments with genome editing to increase effective HR, but initially it is necessary to perform additional research the fundamental aspects of its functioning.
